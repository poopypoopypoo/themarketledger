Meta printed $201 billion in revenue in 2025, growing 22% year over year, with 41% operating margins and earnings per share of $23.49. And yet the stock is sitting 28% below its August 2025 all-time high of $796, with the market broadly spooked by the scale of the company's AI infrastructure spending. At roughly 24x trailing earnings, you are buying one of the best businesses on the planet at a below-market multiple. That kind of setup does not come around often.

Investment thesis
1
The ad business is compounding faster than the market thinks. Meta crossed $200 billion in annual revenue last year, growing 22%, and Q4 2025 alone came in at $59.9 billion, ahead of consensus. Q1 2026 guidance of $53.5 to $56.5 billion is again ahead of what analysts expected. The Advantage+ AI ad suite is at a $60 billion annual run rate and driving measurable improvement in return on ad spend for advertisers, which is the metric that keeps budgets sticky. When advertisers see better results, they spend more. That feedback loop is working.
2
You are paying a below-average multiple for an above-average business. At roughly 24x trailing earnings, Meta trades at a discount to the S&P 500 average, despite significantly faster earnings growth and 41% operating margins. Alphabet trades at 20x. Microsoft trades at 33x. On a price-to-earnings-growth basis, Meta is one of the cheapest mega-cap technology names in the world right now. The 28% drawdown from the August 2025 high is a valuation dislocation, not a sign of anything fundamentally wrong with the business.
3
AI is adding revenue, not just cost. Meta's in-house Llama model cuts external API dependency and is embedded across all its consumer products. Threads hit 400 million monthly active users in late 2025, up from 320 million the year prior, and monetization has not even begun yet. Meta is also projected to surpass Alphabet in global digital advertising revenue by end of 2026. The market is treating the $115 to $135 billion capex guide as a red flag. I think it is the reason the stock is worth more in three years than it is today.
Key catalysts
Q1 2026 earnings
April 29 report. First real test of Advantage+ AI ad revenue at scale. A beat here likely closes part of the valuation gap quickly.
Advertising share shift
Meta is projected to surpass Alphabet in global digital ad revenue by end of 2026. If that happens on schedule, expect a meaningful re-rating.
Threads monetization
400 million MAUs and still no ads. When Threads turns on monetization, it is pure incremental revenue with near-zero incremental cost.
Risks worth watching
Capex discipline: The $115 to $135 billion 2026 capex guide is a big number, up roughly 73% from the $72 billion spent in 2025. If AI monetization does not start showing up clearly in margins by late 2026, investors will lose patience and the multiple will compress further.
Reality Labs losses: The division has now accumulated $83.6 billion in cumulative losses, with another $19 billion expected in 2026. There is no near-term path to profitability here. It is a long-term bet that Zuckerberg is making with shareholder capital, and not everyone is comfortable with that.
Regulatory and legal exposure: The FTC antitrust case and ongoing legal battles across Europe and multiple US states are live overhangs. None of these are likely to cause existential damage, but they create headline risk and could cap the multiple at certain points.
