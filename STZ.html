Constellation Brands owns Corona, Modelo Especial, and Pacifico and three of the most recognizable beer brands in the United States and and the stock is down roughly 40% from its highs on tariff fears and a one-time wine portfolio writedown. The beer business has not changed. Beer margins remain at 38%, the company beat both revenue and EPS estimates on its most recent April 8 earnings report, and the comparable EPS for FY2026 came in at $9.62. At roughly 17x trailing comparable earnings against a peer group trading at 18.8x and a DCF fair value north of $300, the market is pricing in permanent damage to a franchise that has consistently grown for over a decade. I think that is wrong, and the entry point here is one of the more compelling risk/reward setups in consumer staples today.

Investment thesis
1
The selloff is fear-driven, not fundamentals-driven. STZ has fallen roughly 40% from its highs primarily on two concerns: tariff exposure on Mexican beer imports, and softness in Modelo depletion volumes. Both are real, but the market has extrapolated them into permanent structural damage. The beer business posted 38% operating margins in FY2026 and beat both EPS and revenue estimates on April 8. Comparable EPS came in at $9.62 for the year. The wine and spirits goodwill writedown that pressured the reported figures was a one-time accounting item, not an operating deterioration. The core beer franchise is not broken.
2
Corona and Modelo are among the most durable brand franchises in U.S. beverage. Modelo Especial has been the top-selling beer in the United States by dollar share for several years. Corona Extra remains the leading imported beer. These are not trends and they are deeply embedded consumer habits across a growing Hispanic demographic that represents one of the fastest-expanding consumer segments in the country. The pricing power attached to these brands is real and has held up under tariff and inflationary pressure alike. Constellation has beaten earnings estimates in three of the last four quarters with surprise magnitudes ranging from 7% to 16%.
3
The valuation does not reflect the quality of the underlying business. At roughly 17x comparable earnings against a beverage peer group at 18.8x, STZ is trading at a discount to peers despite having superior beer margins and a stronger brand portfolio than most of the names above it in valuation. The DCF-implied fair value is approximately $309 per share based on projected free cash flow through 2035. Even using the more conservative analyst consensus target of $176, the stock offers meaningful upside from current levels while you collect a 2.5% dividend. Evercore ISI added STZ to its Tactical Outperform list ahead of the April 8 earnings specifically citing this valuation gap.
Key catalysts
April 8 earnings beat
Beat EPS by 9.6% and revenue estimates on the most recent print. Execution is holding up despite the macro noise.
Tariff resolution
Any easing of U.S.-Mexico tariff pressure removes the single biggest overhang on the stock and would likely trigger a rapid re-rating toward peer multiples.
Wine and spirits exit
STZ has been actively shedding its underperforming wine and spirits portfolio to become a pure-play beer company. Each divestiture simplifies the story and improves the margin profile.
Risks worth watching
Tariff escalation: Constellation's entire beer portfolio is brewed in Mexico. A sustained increase in U.S.-Mexico tariffs would directly compress margins and could pressure the investment thesis if the company cannot pass costs through pricing.
Hispanic consumer pressure: A meaningful portion of Modelo and Corona volume comes from Hispanic consumers, who are disproportionately exposed to immigration policy uncertainty and wage pressure. Any sustained pullback in that demographic would show up in depletion volumes.
Modelo volume softness: Recent depletion data has shown some slowdown in Modelo volume growth. If this is the beginning of a trend rather than a temporary pause, it undermines the core bull case. The April 8 results suggested stabilization, but the next few quarters will be telling.
