Investment thesis
1
The guidance cut is a fuel story, not a demand story. United lowered full-year EPS guidance from $12 to $14 down to $7 to $11 entirely because of a $340 million year-over-year increase in fuel costs driven by the Iran war. Jet fuel peaked at $4.78 a gallon on April 2 and has since fallen to $3.51 as Iran reopened the Strait of Hormuz. United's own guidance explicitly states it expects to recover 85 to 100% of fuel cost increases by Q4. If fuel stays near current levels, the company is tracking toward the upper half of the range and which is still a very profitable year. The market is treating a temporary external shock as a permanent impairment.
2
The premium revenue engine is structurally stronger than the headline suggests. Q1 premium revenue was up 14% year-over-year. Loyalty revenue grew 13%. Basic economy grew 7%. Corporate travel reached 95% of 2019 levels with contracted corporate revenues up 13%. MileagePlus credit card spend hit a new record. United is not a commodity airline and it has diversified its revenue base into high-margin streams that are far less sensitive to fuel costs than traditional ticket revenue. The United Next fleet strategy, with MAX deliveries running ahead of schedule, also adds a structural margin tailwind as newer, more fuel-efficient planes enter service.
3
The valuation does not reflect the quality of the business. At roughly 10x forward earnings with a fair P/E estimate of 17x, a PEG ratio of 0.65, and an average analyst price target of $130 implying 28% upside, UAL is the cheapest it has been on a fundamental basis in years. Return on equity is 24%. Free cash flow was $2.56 billion over the trailing twelve months. The consensus rating is Strong Buy across 13 analysts. This is a business with real earnings power being discounted because of a temporary commodity shock that is already reversing.
Key catalysts
Fuel price normalization
Jet fuel has already fallen from $4.78 to $3.51. Further declines push United toward the upper half of its $7 to $11 EPS guidance range.
Q2 2026 earnings
Any sign that fuel recovery is tracking ahead of the 40 to 50% Q2 assumption would be a meaningful positive catalyst for the stock.
Fleet modernization
MAX deliveries running ahead of schedule add fuel efficiency gains that structurally reduce cost per available seat mile over time.
Risks worth watching
Fuel re-escalation: If geopolitical tensions reignite and oil prices spike again, United's second-half recovery thesis breaks down. The company's guidance explicitly flags this as the key binary risk.
Macro and demand softening: Consumer confidence hit a record low this week. If leisure travel demand weakens materially alongside already-pressured low-income consumers, volume assumptions could disappoint even if fuel cooperates.
Debt load: United carries $31 billion in debt against $12.2 billion in cash, giving a net debt position of roughly $18.8 billion. In a prolonged stress scenario, the balance sheet limits flexibility in a way that pure-play growth businesses do not face.
Labor negotiations: Ongoing negotiations with various labor groups represent a cost wildcard that has not yet been fully resolved. An unfavorable settlement would add to the already elevated cost per available seat mile.
